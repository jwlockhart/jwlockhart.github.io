UMich's Computational Social Science Course

Posted on Mon 18 February 2019 in heuristics

I'm part of a team1 designing the University of Michigan's first Computational Social Science course. The curriculum has been two years in the making, and this Fall 2019 we will enroll our first set of students! All of the course materials are or will soon be on the course GitHub, free for anyone to use. Expect changes and updates as we learn from our experience teaching the course.

About the class

The class is designed for first year undergraduate students with no background in either social or computer science. We're targeting this audience for two reasons: first, we hope it will foster interests the students can pursue during the rest of their degrees. Second, by teaching without prerequisites, we aim to remove barriers to entry that often plague computational social science. We are also designing a graduate level variant of the same class, again with minimal prerequisites.

We are committed to giving students hands-on experience interacting with code, using real CSS tools with real world data about phenomena of sociological interest. Therefore, we designed it as a lab-based class like those found in STEM fields. That's a tall order for a beginner class, and it does unfortunately mean that the students will be interacting with and tweaking code we wrote rather than writing their own code from scratch.

Hands on lessons and example code

The course has weekly lab exercises on a variety of CSS topics, complete with example code and data. Here's our list of units and topics from the GitHub:

  • CSSLabs-NLP
    • Intro to python and text data
    • Data munging
    • Word frequencies
    • Topic modeling
  • CSSLabs-Experiments
    • Understanding Salganik & Watts' Music Lab
    • Recreating Music Lab
  • CSSLabs_Community_Dynamics
    • Introduction to Reddit data (using college sports subreddits)
    • Evolution of community norms (using r/TwoXChromosomes)
    • Other shocks to communities
  • Drawing-Conclusions
    • Inferences and generalizability (sampling, testing, evaluating)
    • Algorithms & Society: ethics and social consequences
  • CSSLabs-Networks
    • Introduction to networks
    • Network structure
    • Social rank & hierarchy
  • CSSLabs-Contagion
    • Agent based models
    • The spread of #metoo on twitter (using 12 months of nearly complete #metoo data)
  • Geospatial analysis
    • Featuring NYC Taxi trip data
